# Law of Uniform Connectedness

> Visually connected elements read as more related than unconnected ones, overriding proximity and similarity.

## Key Takeaways

- Lines, arrows, and shared enclosures bind elements more strongly than spacing or styling alone.
- Useful for diagrams, step flows, and sub-items under a parent — the connector makes the relationship explicit.
- Use sparingly. Over-connecting adds visual noise and competes with simpler grouping cues.
